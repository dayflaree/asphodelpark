About Asphodel Park

Asphodel Park was founded in 1932 in the wooded outskirts near Yellow Creek State Park in Indiana County, Pennsylvania. Its origins trace back to 1924, when three siblings from a close-knit family opened The Shattered Hall, a small rented theater where they staged circus acts and live performances. The success of those shows inspired the siblings to expand, eventually transforming their vision into a full amusement park.

From its opening day, Asphodel Park blended classic entertainment with bold attractions, including the reimagined Shattered Hall as the park's central theater. Over the decades, it has grown into a beloved destination, home to thrilling rides, expansive arcades, and family-friendly spectacles.

Today, Asphodel Park continues to operate under the stewardship of the founding family, welcoming guests from across the country and maintaining its reputation as a thriving, joyful escape.
